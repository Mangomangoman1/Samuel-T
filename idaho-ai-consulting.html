How consulting works

Three sessions, one roadmap, working systems.

A typical consulting engagement runs 1–2 weeks. You text Actrix to start. We schedule the sessions, do the work between calls, and deliver a written plan plus the first agent installed and running. No billing by the hour, no scope creep.

Session one: audit and discovery

We talk through your business operations, customer flow, tools you already use, and the bottlenecks that cost the most time or money. Actrix takes notes, asks clarifying questions, and identifies the highest-value AI opportunities.

60–90 minutes, remote or phone

Session two: roadmap and recommendations

Actrix presents a written assessment and technology roadmap. We walk through each recommendation, discuss trade-offs, and agree on what to build first. You ask questions, push back where needed, and leave with a clear priority list.

Deliverable: written roadmap document

Build and go-live: the first system

Actrix builds the highest-priority agent or workflow identified in the roadmap. You test with real examples. Adjustments are made. The system goes live. From there, you continue to add systems from the roadmap at your own pace.

FAQ

Plain answers about AI consulting.

Most business owners have reasonable skepticism about AI consultants. The industry is full of expensive slide decks and vague promises. Here is what Actrix actually delivers.

What does AI consulting from Actrix include?

Actrix AI consulting covers readiness assessment, technology roadmap, tool evaluation, custom AI policy development, and implementation planning. Each engagement is scoped to the business — no package upsell, no one-size-fits-all plan. The engagement ends with a working system installed in your business, not just a document.

How is Actrix different from other AI consultants?

Actrix specializes in real Idaho businesses, not enterprise slide decks. We focus on operational AI that actually gets installed — missed call rescue, quote intake, follow-up automation, owner insight agents. The consulting leads to working systems, not just strategy documents. We also do not charge by the hour or sell annual retainers.

Do I need to know what AI tools I want before consulting?

No. That is the point of the consulting engagement. Actrix assesses your current operations, identifies the highest-value automation opportunities, and recommends specific tools, agents, and workflows. You do not need to be a technical expert — you just need to know what parts of your business feel slow or repetitive.

What if my business already uses AI but it is scattered?

That is common and the consulting audit is designed for exactly this situation. Actrix reviews every AI tool and automation you currently use, identifies overlaps, gaps, and risks, and recommends a consolidated approach. The goal is fewer tools doing more useful work, with clear guidelines for staff.

Is consulting available if I am not in the Wood River Valley?

Yes. Actrix serves Idaho businesses statewide. Consulting sessions happen remotely or by phone. The same assessment, roadmap, and build process works whether you are in Boise, Idaho Falls, Coeur d'Alene, Twin Falls, Pocatello, Moscow, or anywhere else in Idaho.

How long does a consulting engagement take?

A typical consulting engagement runs 1–2 weeks. It includes 2–3 sessions, a written assessment and roadmap, and the installation of the first priority AI system. The timeline depends on complexity, but most businesses have a working agent within days of approving the plan.
