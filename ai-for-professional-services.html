The problem

Your firm loses revenue every time a client waits.

Professional service firms run on responsiveness and trust. When inquiries go cold, follow-up slips, and admin work piles up, it directly hits your bottom line. These are not small annoyances — they are structural drag on a billable-hour business.

📞

Missed inquiries steal clients

A potential client calls at 4:30 PM on a Friday. You are in a closing, a deposition, or a client meeting. They leave a voicemail, wait 48 hours, and call the next firm on their list. That is a client you paid to acquire and then lost to silence.

📋

Follow-up falls to the busy

The partner intends to follow up. The associate means to send that engagement letter. But nobody has a dedicated person for client follow-up. It gets pushed until it feels awkward — and then it never happens.

📊

Admin eats billable hours

Every hour a paralegal spends screening inquiry calls or a partner spends typing the same intro email is an hour not spent on billable work or high-value client relationships. The overhead is invisible until you count it.

FAQ

Straight answers for professional firms.

Does the agent pretend to be a lawyer or CPA?

No. The agent identifies itself clearly as an AI assistant working with your firm. It says something like “This is the client intake assistant for Smith & Associates. Our team is in a meeting — I can help gather the details so they can get back to you quickly.” Transparency builds trust.

What about confidentiality and data security?

Client conversations stay encrypted. The agent does not store case details, financial information, or personal data beyond what it needs to route the inquiry. All client communication flows to your review inbox — nothing is shared externally. We can sign your standard confidentiality agreement.

Can the agent handle multiple practice areas or office locations?

Yes. The Full Practice System routes inquiries by practice area (family law, estate planning, corporate, tax, etc.) or office location. Each area gets its own script, intake questions, and routing rules. The partner or lead for that area receives the brief.

What happens with complex client questions?

The agent handles common questions (office hours, service areas, initial consultation process, document requirements). For anything specific or complex, it says “Let me get the right team member to answer this — they will follow up with you shortly” and routes it to you as a priority note.

How do I approve responses before they go out?

The agent drafts every response and sends it to your phone for review. You approve, edit, or reject with a tap. Nothing goes to a client without your OK. You can set business-hours rules so responses send automatically during office hours and queue for your review after hours.

Do I need to install software or train my staff?

No. Everything works through text messages and a simple review link. If you can send a text, you can use the system. No app store, no login, no staff training. Your paralegals and associates do not need to learn anything new.

Can this work with my existing CRM or scheduling system?

Usually yes. The agent can integrate with common practice management tools, CRMs, and calendar systems. We map the integration during the setup session so leads captured by the agent flow into the systems you already use.

How fast can we get this running?

Most professional service firms are live within one to two weeks of the first conversation. The intake call takes about 45 minutes. We build your scripts, test them against real scenarios, and launch with a soft go-live before full deployment.
