The problem

Every interruption costs you billable time and customer trust.

Running a repair shop means juggling the work on your bench and the people waiting for answers. When the phone rings every twenty minutes for a status update, you are not fixing anything. But ignoring those calls costs you repeat business.

📞

Status calls interrupt the work

“Is my phone ready yet?” “Did you find out what is wrong with my truck?” Every status call costs you ten minutes of focus. On a busy day, that is an hour of labor lost to phone tag — and the customer is no happier for having called twice.

📦

Intake is slow and inconsistent

A walk-in drops off a device. You grab a sticky note and scribble the issue. Half the time, the problem description is wrong or missing by the time it reaches the bench. No standardized intake means missed details, wrong estimates, and angry customers.

📋

Follow-up slips through the cracks

The part came in. The repair is done. But you are buried in the next job, so the customer sits waiting. A week later they leave a one-star review saying you never called them back. The work was good — but the communication was not.

How it works

From drop-off to pick-up, the agent handles the conversation.

The agent works through text messages and a simple approval link. No app to install, no dashboard to check, no training for your team. It picks up the calls you miss, talks to customers for you, and hands you the information you actually need.

Intake captured automatically

A customer calls or walks in. The agent collects the device details, problem description, contact info, and preferred pickup time. It standardizes every intake so your bench gets the same level of detail on every job — no more sticky notes.

Complete intake brief in minutes

Status updates, no phone tag

When you update a job status in your system, the agent texts the customer automatically: “Your iPhone is in diagnostic. We found a battery issue. Estimated cost: $89. Would you like us to proceed?” The customer replies yes or no, and the job moves forward.

Customers stay informed without calling

Approvals and follow-up handled

The agent sends repair estimates for your review, asks the customer to approve pricing, sends pickup reminders when the job is done, and follows up three days later to make sure everything is working. Every conversation happens by text — logged and traceable.

FAQ

Straight answers for shop owners.

Does the agent answer phone calls or just send texts?

It texts the caller back immediately with a friendly introduction and starts a conversation. It does not use a robot voice to answer calls. The goal is fast, warm communication with clean information — not an automated phone tree your customers will hate.

Can the agent give customers an estimate over text?

The agent does not generate estimates on its own — you set the pricing. When a diagnostic is complete, the agent sends the repair cost you provide and asks the customer to approve or decline by reply. You stay in control of pricing; the agent just delivers the message and captures the decision.

How do status updates work? Does the agent know when a repair is done?

You update the job status in your system (or text a keyword), and the agent notifies the customer. No extra work for you — the same update you would normally track becomes an automatic customer text. Status updates include: received, in diagnostic, awaiting parts, repair in progress, ready for pickup.

What if a customer has a complex technical question?

The agent handles common questions (hours, drop-off process, pricing range, turnaround time, warranty info). For specific technical questions about a repair, it says “Let me get our technician to answer this — they will follow up shortly” and routes it to you as a priority message.

How does part-order approval work?

When a part needs ordering, the agent texts the customer: “We found the issue. The replacement part costs $X and will arrive in Y days. Would you like us to order it?” The customer replies Yes or No. If Yes, the part is ordered and the agent texts an updated ETA. No back-and-forth phone calls required.

Do I need to install software or learn a new system?

No. Everything works through text messages and a simple review link. If you can send a text, you can use the system. No app to install, no login to remember, no staff training. Your existing phone and job management are all you need.

Can this work with my existing shop management software?

Usually yes. The agent can integrate with common shop management tools, POS systems, and job tracking software. We map the integration during setup so new jobs and status updates flow between the agent and the systems you already use.

How fast can I get this running?

Most repair shops are live within one week of the first conversation. The setup call takes about 45 minutes. We build your scripts and intake questions, test with real customer scenarios, and launch with a soft go-live before full deployment.
