What training covers

Four modules, one goal: staff who use AI tools well and safely.

Every training session is built from the same core modules but customized to your business. The tools you use, the work your team does, the risks that matter to your industry — the training adapts to all of them.

Module 1

AI tool skills: prompt writing and output evaluation

Hands-on practice with ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or custom AI agents. Staff learn how to write prompts that get useful results, how to evaluate output for accuracy and tone, and how to edit AI drafts without starting from scratch. Each session uses examples from your actual business — real emails, real customer questions, real workflows.

Practical, not academic
Module 2

AI safety, data privacy, and human review

Clear rules for what staff can and cannot do with AI tools. Which customer information is safe to paste into ChatGPT. What requires human approval before sending. How to spot hallucinations, false confidence, and tone problems. Every team member gets the same plain-language guidelines, so the whole business stays consistent.

Protects your business from AI mistakes
Module 3

Workflow adoption: AI in your actual operations

Training tied to your specific workflows — not generic exercises. If your team handles quote requests, the training uses real quote scenarios. If they manage missed calls, the training walks through the rescue workflow. The goal is not to teach AI in isolation but to embed it into the work your team already does every day.

Customized to your business
Module 4

AI literacy: understanding capabilities and limits

Plain-language explanations of how AI works, where it is reliable, where it fails, and how to stay in control. Staff learn the difference between generative AI, automation, and search. They understand why AI sometimes invents facts, why it cannot do math reliably, and how to use it as a tool rather than an oracle.

Builds confidence and skepticism in equal measure

FAQ

Plain answers about AI training.

Staff training raises reasonable questions. Will it take too much time? Will skeptical staff tune out? Is training really necessary if the tools are supposed to be easy? Here are the honest answers.

What does AI training from Actrix include?

Actrix AI training covers AI tool skills (ChatGPT, Claude, and custom agents), safe AI use and data protection, workflow adoption for your specific tools and systems, custom agent training for Actrix-built systems, and basic AI literacy for non-technical staff. Each training session is tailored to your business — no generic curriculum, no certification exam, no ongoing subscription required.

Do I need a technical background to attend training?

No. Actrix training is designed for non-technical staff. We assume no prior AI experience. The training starts with what AI can and cannot do, then moves to practical tool use, and ends with your specific workflows. Everything is taught in plain language with live demonstrations and hands-on practice. Your bookkeeper, receptionist, project manager, and owner can all attend the same session and get value.

How long does a typical training session take?

Most training sessions run 60–90 minutes. A full team onboarding may take 2–3 sessions over a week. Follow-up sessions are shorter — 30–45 minutes — and focus on real scenarios your staff encountered between sessions. Most teams are comfortable with core tools after one session and proficient after two. We do not sell training hours; we sell skills that stick.

Can training happen remotely or on-site?

Both options are available. Remote training works well for most teams — Actrix shares screens, walks through real examples, and answers questions live. On-site training is available for Idaho businesses within reasonable travel distance from the Wood River Valley. Remote is the standard option; on-site can be arranged for larger teams or specialized needs.

Do you train staff on tools they already have?

Yes. If your team already uses ChatGPT, Claude, Google Gemini, Microsoft Copilot, or other AI tools, Actrix can audit current usage and provide targeted training to fix bad habits, improve prompting techniques, tighten data safety, and get better results from tools you already pay for. We do not try to sell you new tools unless your current stack has a clear gap.

What if my staff are skeptical about AI?

Skepticism is common and healthy. Actrix does not hype AI or pressure adoption. Training starts with honest limitations — what AI is bad at, where it makes mistakes, what data should never go into a public tool, why human review matters. Staff who understand the risks are more likely to use the tools safely and effectively than staff who were told AI is magic. Skepticism becomes a strength once it has accurate information to work with.

Is AI training available outside the Wood River Valley?

Yes. Actrix serves Idaho businesses statewide. Remote training works for any team with an internet connection, regardless of location. The same curriculum, approach, and quality applies whether you are in Boise, Idaho Falls, Coeur d'Alene, Twin Falls, Pocatello, Moscow, Rexburg, or anywhere else in Idaho. On-site training may be arranged for larger groups.
