# Actrix Idaho SEO Sprint Playbook

This is the non-code side of the sprint. The site can clarify relevance, but local rankings also need prominence: real mentions, reviews, profiles, proof, and consistent business information across the web.

## Priority 1: Google Business Profile

Set up or fully complete the Google Business Profile as a service-area business. Use a real verified business identity and do not add fake offices.

Suggested profile language:

> Actrix helps Idaho businesses install practical AI automation: custom agents, missed-call rescue, quote intake, AI support, workflow automation, safe staff AI training, and owner-approved systems.

Suggested services to add:

- AI automation
- AI consulting
- Business automation
- Workflow automation
- Custom AI agents
- AI training
- AI support
- Missed-call rescue
- Quote intake automation

Review request prompt:

> Could you leave a short Google review for Actrix? The most helpful thing to mention is the workflow we helped with, the Idaho area your business serves, and what changed after the system was installed.

## Priority 2: Proof Assets

Publish one proof item every week for the next 8 weeks. It does not need to be a huge case study.

Proof formats:

- Before/after workflow map.
- "What we automated this week" field note.
- Short Loom/video walkthrough.
- Screenshot of a sanitized decision brief.
- Owner lesson from a messy AI workflow.
- FAQ post answering one buyer objection.

Target proof topics:

- Custom AI agent stack implementation for an Idaho company.
- Workflow integration across inboxes, forms, CRM, calendars, docs, and reports.
- Operations intelligence brief that surfaces weak spots from daily business data.
- Missed-call rescue as the first agent in a broader sales stack.
- Quote intake for home services.
- AI support cleanup for a staff team.
- Safe AI policy for a business preparing to use agents.

## Priority 3: Local Mentions And Citations

Build legitimate Idaho presence signals. Do not buy spam links.

Targets:

- Local chamber profiles where relevant.
- Idaho business directories.
- Boise, Wood River Valley, Twin Falls, Idaho Falls, and Coeur d'Alene startup/business communities.
- Podcast guest spots with Idaho business or tech shows.
- Local partner pages from web designers, IT providers, marketing agencies, and consultants.
- Sponsorships or small event pages that list Actrix by name and link to the site.

Outreach angle:

> I run Actrix, an Idaho AI implementation firm building custom AI agent stacks, workflow integrations, and operations intelligence systems for Idaho companies. I made a plain-English buyer guide and ROI calculator for local owners evaluating AI implementation. Would this be useful for your members or clients?

## Priority 4: Search Console Tracking

Weekly checks:

- Queries moving from positions 70-90 toward 40-60.
- Pages getting impressions but no clicks.
- Pages with high impressions for "AI automation Idaho," "AI consulting Idaho," "AI for business Idaho," "AI automation Boise," and city terms.
- Whether Google chooses the intended title and description.
- Internal pages with zero impressions after indexing.

Decision rule:

- If a page gets impressions but low clicks, rewrite the title and first-screen copy.
- If a page gets no impressions after 30 days, add internal links from the homepage, Idaho hub, and site map.
- If a query appears often, create or improve a page that directly answers the intent.

## Priority 5: Review And Link Flywheel

Ask every legitimate customer, collaborator, or early workflow tester for one of three proof signals:

- Google review.
- LinkedIn recommendation or post.
- Website mention with a link to the most relevant Actrix page.

Best phrases to naturally earn in reviews:

- AI implementation for my Idaho business.
- Custom AI agent stack.
- Workflow integration.
- Operations intelligence.
- AI automation.
- Missed calls.
- Quote intake.
- Custom AI agent.
- Staff AI training.
- Practical AI support.

Do not script reviews. Ask for honest specifics.
