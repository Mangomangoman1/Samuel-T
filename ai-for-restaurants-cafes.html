How it works

Three steps from missed call to confirmed reservation.

The agent does not pretend to be your host. It collects what matters, organizes the details, and hands you a booking ready to confirm — without anyone touching the phone during service.

The call lands on the agent

When you miss a call, the guest gets a warm, fast text back instead of voicemail. The agent introduces itself, says you are in service, and asks what they need — a reservation, a question, or catering.

Agent gathers the booking

Party size, time, seating preference, dietary needs, and contact info. The agent works the conversation like an experienced host — thorough, warm, and never rushed. Special requests get noted.

You review and confirm

A clean reservation brief lands in your phone. You check the details, adjust if needed, and approve. The guest gets a warm confirmation — even though you were in the weeds during dinner service.

FAQ

Straight answers for restaurant owners.

Does the agent answer calls or just text?

It texts the caller back immediately with a warm, natural message and starts a conversation. It does not pretend to be your host on the phone. The goal is fast response and clean information — not a robot voice during dinner service.

What if the guest has a special request or allergy question?

The agent captures dietary restrictions, allergies, and preferences in the booking flow. For complex questions (specific ingredients, customization), it says “I will have the chef follow up on that” and routes it to you as a priority note.

Do I need to install anything or learn software?

No. Everything works through text messages and a simple review link. If you can send a text, you can use the system. No app store, no login, no training. Your guests do not need to install anything either.

What kind of restaurants and cafes does this work for?

Full-service restaurants, cafes, bars, breweries, wine bars, pizzerias, bakeries, food trucks with reservation or catering traffic, and any hospitality business where the phone rings when nobody is at the host stand.

Does this replace my host or front-of-house staff?

No. The best first use is catching what falls through the cracks: calls during dinner rush, after-hours booking inquiries, unanswered reviews, and catering leads that disappear because nobody follows up. If you have a host stand, this gives your team better-prepared leads. If you do not have one, it fills the gap.

How fast can I get this running?

Most restaurants are up and running within a week of the first conversation. We tailor the scripts to your menu style, hours, reservation flow, and the questions your guests actually ask.
