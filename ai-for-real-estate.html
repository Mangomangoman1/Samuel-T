The problem

In real estate, speed is the difference between a sale and a missed opportunity.

Buyers and sellers expect instant responses. Every hour you wait to reply, a competitor picks up the phone. Idaho's market moves fast — and the agents who respond first get the walkthrough, the offer, and the commission.

📞

After-hours inquiries go cold

The majority of online listing views happen after 5 PM and on weekends. That is exactly when most agents are off the clock. Without a response system, those leads call the next name on Zillow by morning.

📋

Qualification takes too long

Back-and-forth texts to figure out budget, timeline, pre-approval status, and must-haves waste hours. By the time you have the basics, the buyer has already booked a showing with a faster agent.

📅

Follow-up falls through the cracks

You showed the house. You said you would follow up. Then you had three more showings, a listing appointment, and a closing. The buyer never heard from you again. That is a commission you earned and then lost to silence.

FAQ

Straight answers for real estate pros.

Does the agent answer calls or just send texts?

It texts the caller back immediately with a friendly introduction and starts a conversation. It does not use a robot voice to answer live calls. The goal is fast, warm response with clean information — not a script reading over the phone.

Can the agent handle multiple listings or property types?

Yes. In the Full Pipeline System, the agent asks which property the buyer is calling about and routes the inquiry to the right agent or response script. It works across residential, commercial, land, rental properties, and vacation homes — each with its own set of qualifying questions.

What if the buyer has specific questions I need to answer personally?

The agent handles common questions: price, square footage, HOA details, school zones, showing availability, and financing basics. For complex questions about property history, disclosures, or negotiation strategy, it says “Let me connect you with my agent — they know this property inside out” and routes it to you as a priority note.

How does showing scheduling work?

You connect your calendar (Google Calendar, iCal, or Outlook). The agent checks your available time slots, offers the buyer 2-3 options, confirms the showing, sends a calendar invite to both parties, and sets a reminder for you 30 minutes before. No back-and-forth emails required.

Can this work with my existing CRM or IDX?

Usually yes. The agent can integrate with common real estate tools like kvCore, Follow Up Boss, Chime, BoomTown, and others. We map the integration during setup so captured leads and activity flow into the systems you already use.

What does “owner review” mean for real estate?

Every response the agent drafts goes to you for approval before it is sent. You review the lead brief — buyer details, questions asked, qualification answers — and approve, edit, or reject with a tap. Nothing goes to a buyer without your OK. You are always in control.

Do I need to install software or learn a new system?

No. Everything works through text messages and a simple review link. If you can send a text, you can use the system. No app to install, no login to remember, no staff training. Your existing phone and calendar are all you need.

How fast can I get this running?

Most real estate professionals are live within one week of the first conversation. The setup call takes about 45 minutes. We build your scripts, connect your calendar, test with real scenarios, and launch with a soft go-live before full deployment.
